I’m Drishty Jadia, a physics student from IIT Kanpur with a deep curiosity about the universe and the tools we use to study it. Over the past few years, I’ve found myself drawn toward the skies — not just figuratively, but through work in radio astronomy, high-energy astrophysics, and citizen science. What drives me is the process of learning by doing: piecing together radio transients across survey epochs, analyzing X-ray jets from distant galaxies, and spending nights debugging data pipelines until things finally align. I’ve had the chance to work on large-scale datasets from VLA, Chandra, and LOFAR, and these experiences have only made me more eager to ask better questions. I’m also someone who believes science doesn’t exist in isolation — I’ve shared this love for the sky through informal skywatching sessions and by contributing to projects like the Comet Catchers and the International Asteroid Search Campaign. Every dataset, every false positive, every quiet hour spent with DS9 or CASA has taught me something new about persistence and joy in scientific work. I hope to pursue a PhD in astrophysics abroad, where I can continue exploring galaxies, variability, and instrumentation, and where I can grow into the kind of researcher who builds, questions, and mentors. I don’t yet know exactly where this journey leads, but I’m deeply committed to walking it with curiosity and resilience.
