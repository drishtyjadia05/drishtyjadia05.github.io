I’m Drishty Jadia, a physics student from IIT Kanpur with a deep curiosity about the universe and the tools we use to study it. Over the past few years, I’ve found myself drawn toward the skies — not just figuratively, but through work in radio astronomy, high-energy astrophysics, and citizen science.

What drives me is the process of learning by doing: piecing together radio transients across survey epochs, analyzing X-ray jets from distant galaxies, and spending nights debugging data pipelines until things finally align.

I hope to pursue a PhD in astrophysics abroad, where I can continue exploring galaxies, variability, and instrumentation — and grow into a researcher who builds, questions, and mentors with clarity and curiosity.
